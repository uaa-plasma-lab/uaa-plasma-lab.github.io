# Multipole Plasma Trap (MPT)

In the experimental and computational Multipole Plasma Trap research,
radio frequency (RF) multipole electric fields are used to trap low-mass
particles in the plasma, and these in turn trap oppositely charged
high-mass particles. The resulting confined quasi-neutral plasma is a
good test bed for basic plasma phenomena — especially the interaction of
the RF with the boundary of the plasma.
